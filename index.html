Founder of www: World Wide Web

Introduction

Tim Berners-Lee is a software engineer who invented the Internet (or the World Wide Web) in 1989 while at CERN, the European Laboratory of Particle Physics. He is Director of the World Wide Web Consortium which he founded and Professor at the Department of Computer Science from the University of Oxford. In 2001 he became a Fellow of the Royal Society and in 2004 he was knighted.

In 1994 Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Computer Science Laboratory (LCS) at Boston Massachusetts Institute of Technology. It was formed by companies that wanted to work together to create web standards and also to improve the web. It was quickly agreed that the technology should be free of royalties and patents. Berners-Lee has served as the director of this W3C since it was founded. In 1995, Microsoft produced the first version of Internet Explorer and this commercial browser created huge growth in websites and web users. The Web was used by first time for e-commerce in 1997
